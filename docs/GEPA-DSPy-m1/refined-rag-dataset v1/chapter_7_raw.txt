[CHUNK 01]
CONCEPT: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N
TYPE: formula
REFERENCES: The ABCs of How Emotions are Created (Chapter 3); The Four Blocks to Happiness (Chapter 2)

Depression is probably the most painful negative emotion that human beings experience. There are three major concepts or thought processes that lie behind Depression: Hopelessness, Helplessness, and Need. These three factors can fit into a mathematical equation for Depression as follows:
D = H1 + H2 + N
Depression = Hopelessness + Helplessness + Need

--------------------------------------------------------

[CHUNK 02]
CONCEPT: H1: Hopelessness — Three Components
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; IB6: Absolutistic Statements (Chapter 4)

Hopelessness means that you have decided that your situation is beyond help; that is, that your current position in life is so bad that it will never get any better. There are three components to H1, Hopelessness:
1: A pessimistic or negative outlook toward the past. When you give up on the past, you are thinking that since bad things happened in the past, you are doomed to more bad things for the rest of your life. You think that the negative choices or events that occurred in your past are the cause of your current Depression and since you can't change those, you can't stop being depressed.
2: A pessimistic or negative outlook toward the present. When you give up on the present, you believe that your situation is unchangeable, no matter what you do today.
3: A pessimistic or negative outlook toward the future. Since you can't change the past, and you won't change the present, the future is also bleak and gloomy.

--------------------------------------------------------

[CHUNK 03]
CONCEPT: H2: Helplessness — Three Components
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; IB5: Rating (Chapter 4); IB2: Awfulizing (Chapter 4)

There are three components to H2, Helplessness:
1: Self-Pity — When you experience self-pity, you feel bad for yourself and you throw yourself a pity party and invite all your worse friends. There is nothing wrong with grieving when something happens that you perceive as bad. It's when you take it to the next level, telling yourself things like, 'Nothing ever works out the way I want it to,' or 'life is always unfair to me,' and that life is useless and worthless.' You tell yourself that you are helpless. So, poor you, everything is always bad!
2: Other Pity — When you pity the plight of others, you feel bad about things that are outside of your control. You think things like, 'The world is always a bad place, the environment is getting worse all the time, and everything is bad and going to hell in a handbasket!' You tell yourself that the world is an awful place and you are helpless to change it, and you get depressed about your impotence to do anything about it.
3: Self-Rejection — This is the final component of helplessness, the rejection of yourself, or putting yourself down. When you reject yourself, you say things like, 'I didn't get the promotion that I thought I would, so I am an awful failure. I am helpless to be any better than I am, so I am doomed to fail at everything forever. I am worthless.'

[CHUNK 04]
CONCEPT: N: Need (Pseudo-Need) — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; IB4: Shoulds, Musts, and Demands (Chapter 4); IB7: The Idea of Entitlement (Chapter 4)

The N in the Depression formula stands for 'Need.' The Need or Necessity is more complex than the other two components of Depression, but it's simple to identify once you understand it. I put the word 'Need' into quotation marks because it is a pseudo-need, i.e., a false Need. There are only five things we need as humans to remain alive: food, water, shelter, air, and the elimination of waste. Everything outside of these five 'contingent needs' for life is a want. Whatever it is you feel you lack that is at the root of your Depression is not a true need, it is a want. Often we believe that 'Need' is love. While love from others is desirable and enjoyable, it is not necessary for you to continue to exist or for you to exist and be happy if you don't have it. That magical jump from 'It is pleasurable' to 'I need it, I must have it, and I will fail without it,' is where true disturbance lies.

[CHUNK 05]
CONCEPT: Depression Is Unique — Simultaneous vs. Sequential
TYPE: distinction
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; The Formula for Anger — A = ET + S (Chapter 5); The Formula for Anxiety — AX = WI + AW + ICSI (Chapter 6)

Depression is unique because, unlike the other four blocks, hopelessness, helplessness, and need don't necessarily occur in order. Rather, they all occur simultaneously and are linked to one another. Although you may want to look for your need first, you also want to be aware that all three are occurring at once. Depression, like the other blocks to human happiness, is a choice to think and believe in certain ways. There is always the other side of the coin, non-Depression, or acceptance and contentment with the way things are, not as we wish they were.

[CHUNK 06]
CONCEPT: The Four Pseudo-Needs and Four Fears — Framework
TYPE: list
REFERENCES: N: Need (Pseudo-Need); H1: Hopelessness; H2: Helplessness; The Formula for Anxiety — AX = WI + AW + ICSI (Chapter 6)

Every 'Need' is attached to a fear. If we believe we 'need' something in order to be happy, we fear not getting it and that not having the need met will be Intolerable, Awful, and we Couldn't Stand It!
NEED = FEAR
The four pseudo needs are:
1: The 'Need' for love and approval from others = the fear of Rejection.
2: The 'Need' for success = the fear of Failure.
3: The 'Need' for comfort = the fear of pain and discomfort.
4: The 'Need' for certainty = the fear of uncertainty, ambivalence, and death.
These fears have a certain irony to them, because often having the fear will cause you to behave in such a way that the fear materializes in the mind and body through physiological processes and behavior that becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. When you're afraid of rejection, you don't act confident, and you get rejected. When you fear failure, you are paralyzed when it is time to perform or take a risk. When you fear pain and discomfort, it is practically impossible to escape addiction or unhealthy habits. When you fear uncertainty, you will stay in the same comfortable misery for as long as you have the fear. And this may be forever.

[CHUNK 07]
CONCEPT: Comfortable Misery — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Four Pseudo-Needs and Four Fears; The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; The Formula for Anxiety — AX = WI + AW + ICSI (Chapter 6)

One of the reasons we do not change our belief is that even though we are miserable in our situation we are also familiar with it. I call this comfortable misery. Change involves doing something different and often outside our comfort zone, and we fear they may experience more Anxiety and discomfort than in the present situation. So many people would rather choose unhappiness over uncertainty!

[CHUNK 08]
CONCEPT: Disputing Depression — Method and Finding the Need
TYPE: framework
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; Red-Flagging (Chapter 4); The 4 Steps to Disputing (Chapter 4)

Even in Depression you can change your thought processes through red-flagging and disputing. What can you red-flag for Depression? First, look for the 'Need.' Once you've identified your Need, your need for love and approval for example, it is easier to see where the hopelessness and helplessness come in. Why is understanding the 'Need' component so important? In order for Depression to occur there has to be an underlying need for things to be different. Everything outside the five true or contingent needs is simply a preference or a desire. It's okay to have desires and preferences, but when we cling to and demand them, we get emotionally upset and disturbed. Once you have identified your 'Need,' it is necessary to dispute the Hopelessness and Helplessness that most often follows it. Instead of falling into your negative feedback loop of depressed thoughts and habits (your reactive mind), stop and ask yourself what you think you need and must have right now in order to be happy? Are you really a hopeless person? Is this true or is it just another way of saying 'I give up?'

[CHUNK 09]
CONCEPT: The Mind of Totality — Framework for Confronting Fear
TYPE: framework
REFERENCES: The Four Pseudo-Needs and Four Fears; Why Zen Meditation is Essential Mind Training; Interconnectedness (Chapter 3)

When confronting your fear, try tapping into what Zen calls 'The Mind of Totality.' This simply requires you to zoom out from your narrow, stressed out perspective and look at the big picture. Consider the size of your city or town with the hundreds or thousands of people in it. Widen your view to include your state or province with the millions of people, then your country and the world, with all the millions and billions of other people in it. All of them flawed, all of them temporary. Think of the vastness of the solar system, the galaxy, the universe. Zoom out until you can contemplate the whole of this massive experience, we call life. Realize it is all just a passing show; the pain isn't forever, the pleasure isn't forever. Your ego has never even existed. The mind of totality is your antidote for fear.

[CHUNK 10]
CONCEPT: Practical Tips for Change — Depression Behavioral Protocol
TYPE: list
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; The Centering Breath (Chapter 6); Why Zen Meditation is Essential Mind Training; Healthy Body, Healthy Mind

Changing your thought patterns takes time and practice, but there are a number of practical tips for creating an environment conducive to your work on the mental part:
• Do NOT hibernate (stay at home, in your room, in your bed, etc.).
• Do NOT drink or do so very sparingly. (Alcohol is a depressant. The last thing you need when you're already depressed is to put a chemical in your body that will often lead to more depressed thinking).
• Do NOT watch sad movies or listen to melancholic music (this will only reinforce your already negative mindset).
• DO PYA (Push Your Ass—Albert Ellis again here) to leave the house at least once a day.
• DO talk to and spend time with people who are positive and who you enjoy being around.
• DO get some sunlight and take a walk in nature.
• DO eat healthier. Have some fruits and vegetables and drink 6 to 8 glasses of water every day.
• DO some physical exercise (if you are physically able). Exercise is incredibly beneficial for managing Depression. Almost everyone can do a form of Tai Chi, even if in a wheel chair.
• DO watch and practice meditation at least once a day.
• DO get 8 hours of sleep every night.
• DO practice the Centering Breath when you feel upset.
And finally, love with reckless abandon!

CONCEPT: Medication Nation — Rational Theory vs. Pharmacological Treatment
TYPE: framework
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; The Narrator vs. The Observer (Chapter 1); Disputing — Definition (Chapter 4)

The myriad of medications prescribed to people suffering from Depression are largely unnecessary, ineffective, and accompanied by undesirable side effects. Medication will at best provide only temporary relief, if any at all. Medication is not an effective or permanent solution because it does not address the primary cause of your Depression; your irrational belief system, your non-acceptance of reality as it is. So anti-depressants, anti-psychotics, and anti-anxiety medications are, in essence, anti-reality! Which of these pills can stop or change your thoughts? None of them! Medication can never change your thinking! It cannot shift your conception from Narrator (reactive and irrational) to Observer (active and rational). It can't change your inner dialogue or red-flag irrational beliefs. No matter how comforting the short-term effects, medication will never provide an exit from the Negative Feedback Superhighway! There are no negative side effects or addictions to awareness, to observing, disputing, and releasing irrational beliefs. The only side effect of rational thinking is peace and contentment.

[CHUNK 12]
CONCEPT: Case Example — Woman in Bad Marriage (Depression Formula with 4 Steps)
TYPE: example
REFERENCES: The Formula for Depression — D = H1 + H2 + N; The 4 Steps to Disputing (Chapter 4); Comfortable Misery

A patient felt her marriage was hopeless, that it was beyond repair and certainly doomed. Applying The 4 Steps to Disputing:
STEP 1: OBSERVE — She is feeling frustrated by her lack of control over her disintegrating marriage, feeling depressed about it, hostile towards her husband, etc.
STEP 2: THINK SOS — She is thinking that she must be able to control her marriage, that people will think badly about her if she gets divorced, that her husband should still love her the same way he did when they first got married 17 years ago, that she should be more attractive and smarter, and so on.
STEP 3: IDENTIFY — 'My husband is unhappy and wants a divorce.' This is a true statement that does not over-dramatize what is happening. When you accept the true aspects of a situation, it can help you to recognize what you are exaggerating or Awfulizing about, how you are doing that, and how to dispute it and get through it.
STEP 4: REPLACE — 'I find it very unpleasant to get a divorce,' or 'I'd like our marriage to be happier and to last.' She could accept that her husband had moved on with his life and choose to do the same, or she could do everything she could to save her marriage—pursue couples counseling, start working out and taking classes, and otherwise take better care of herself.
