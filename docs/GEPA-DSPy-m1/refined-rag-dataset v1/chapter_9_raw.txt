[CHUNK 01]
CONCEPT: The Short Formula for Happiness — 100% A = 0% D
TYPE: formula
REFERENCES: Acceptance (Chapter 3); The Four Blocks to Happiness (Chapter 2); The Full ABCDE Model (Chapter 3)

The first equation for happiness is short and simple:
100% A = 0% D
Total Acceptance = Zero Disturbance
The amount of upset that you feel is equal to the number of things that you are not accepting in this moment; the activating event, the other person, yourself, or the situation. So if you accept everything that happens in every moment, you will never be disturbed! If you can accept only 90% of what happens, then you will be 10% emotionally disturbed. If you're 50% accepting, you're 50% disturbed. Whatever numbers you insert, the math always works out. The percentage of the time that you are upset = the percent of the time that you are not accepting things 'as they are' in this present moment.

H = USA + UAOW + SN

[CHUNK 03]
CONCEPT: USA: Unconditional Self-Acceptance — In the Happiness Formula
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Long Formula for Happiness; Unconditional Self-Acceptance (Chapter 8); FHB — Fallible Human Beings (Chapter 6)

Unconditional self-acceptance means that you accept yourself fully and totally, for both your faults and your strengths. It does not, however, mean you don't hold yourself to any standards or that you give up whenever things are too hard. When you screw up, you accept that you are a fallible human being and will make mistakes. You then work hard to do your best whenever possible. Remember that there are no perfect human beings, we are all going to make mistakes many times in our life. It's irrational to expect otherwise of ourselves or anyone else.

--------------------------------------------------------

[CHUNK 04]
CONCEPT: UAOW: Unconditional Acceptance of Others and the World — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Long Formula for Happiness; IB5: Rating (Chapter 4); Acceptance Does Not Mean Endorsement (Chapter 5)

Unconditional acceptance of others and the world as it is (not as we want it to be): the world and the people around us are how they are, and they aren't going to change or be different just because you prefer them to. You will be happier if you let go of your expectations of your coworkers, the other drivers on the highway, or your spouse. They are as they are because all the conditions have occurred for them to be so. It is not possible for them to be any different in this moment. Comparing them to how they might be, or how you wish they were, is indulging in the irrational belief of rating. When you choose to stop rating, you are by definition choosing to practice acceptance. Acceptance of yourself, acceptance of others, and acceptance of the world as it is, all lead to happiness, while rating can only lead to disturbance. Acceptance does not mean be a pushover. You can accept the reality that your coworker took credit for your idea without rating or being upset. You are practicing acceptance and awareness without judgment or endorsement.

[CHUNK 05]
CONCEPT: SN: Staying Now — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: The Long Formula for Happiness; Training the Observer (Chapter 1); Why Zen Meditation is Essential Mind Training; The Formula for Anxiety — AX = WI + AW + ICSI (Chapter 6)

The final component of the longer equation for happiness is staying now. This is a bit of a simplified term for describing 'being in the flow of all of life.' In actuality, you can't stay now, because now, the present moment, has already passed. You are always moving on to a new now! This can also be said as being present or mindful, but it all means the same thing—if you keep your mind in the present and enjoy living each moment, you will be happier and less disturbed. You may be tempted to be anxious about your presentation tomorrow or relive the argument you had with your boss yesterday, but that's wasted energy. You can't change yesterday and worrying won't do anything to change tomorrow. Instead, focus your energy on living here, in the ever-flowing now, and you will be happy. Accept that the past can't be different. All Guilt is hoping for a better past. The future cannot be predicted. Staying now with this constant state of flux, flow, and change is a constant practice. You can allow the flow to pass around you without getting caught up in it or thrashing around to keep your head above water. Detach, be the Observer, and just watch the show.

[CHUNK 07]
CONCEPT: CA: Creative Absorption — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: Greater Happiness Formula — GH = H + CA – HF; Practical Tips for Change (Chapter 7)

Most people who describe themselves as happy have pleasurable and stimulating activities they pursue outside of everyday responsibilities. It is important to occupy, stimulate, recharge, and retrain the mind for optimum function. An activity doesn't have to involve any artistic endeavors. While many people choose painting, music, or learning a foreign language, a creative absorption simply refers to an engaging, pleasurable hobby or activity that you look forward to. It may be organizing things, scuba diving, reading, gardening, or any of thousands of other pastimes. The only qualifier is that it be pleasant to you, and the only limit is your imagination!

[CHUNK 08]
CONCEPT: HF: The Hassle Factor — Definition
TYPE: definition
REFERENCES: Greater Happiness Formula — GH = H + CA – HF; Why Zen Meditation is Essential Mind Training

The hassle factor detracts from happiness and contentment. It can be described as the result of decisions that complicate our lives with physical, mental, or emotional clutter. The hassle factor will cause physical conditions that promote disturbance, so it would be wise to reduce additions that often lead us to becoming stressed. Collecting can be a source of hassle when material things start to take up breathing room and infringe on your physical space and peace of mind. Material things, contrary to what society tells us, are not the source of happiness and contentment, but the very opposite! That's why the Zen monasteries are so spartan—clear, simple, peaceful. My hassle-free motto: 'Less fuss, more peace!'

[CHUNK 09]
CONCEPT: Why Happiness Requires the Four Blocks First — Framework
TYPE: framework
REFERENCES: The Four Blocks to Happiness (Chapter 2); The Formula for Anger (Chapter 5); The Formula for Anxiety (Chapter 6); The Formula for Depression (Chapter 7); The Formula for Guilt (Chapter 8); Why Zen Meditation is Essential Mind Training

If being happy is as simple as two equations, why didn't we just start out with those two? For two reasons. First, because it is important to understand the building blocks of rational thinking to get to a place where you would be able to comprehend these equations and apply them to your life. Unpleasant activating events are inevitably going to happen, and if you are not equipped to deal with these negative events, your happiness will be easily disturbed. Anger, Anxiety, Depression, and Guilt when left undisputed, will most always weigh you down and block you from happiness. Secondly, Happiness takes practice! For this I would highly recommend Zen which I believe just puts the cherry on top of the chocolate ice cream. Far from being some magic or mystical practice, Zen just means meditation. With the tools and knowledge you have acquired, you are now equipped to handle any disturbance you may experience.

[CHUNK 10]
CONCEPT: Case Example — The Golfer (Acceptance Without Endorsement)
TYPE: example
REFERENCES: The Short Formula for Happiness; Acceptance Does Not Mean Endorsement (Chapter 5); Acceptance (Chapter 3)

There was a professional golfer known for both his skill on the golf course and for his charitable spirit. After winning a tournament in South America, he was approached in the parking lot by a younger woman who told him a heart-wrenching story about her infant suffering from a terrible and fatal disease, and the financial pressure she and her husband were suffering to pay the bills. He was so moved that he immediately signed over the check he won in the tournament. The next day he was told that they had tracked down this woman and found she was unmarried and had no child; the golfer had been scammed. He looked at the man and said, 'So you mean there is no dying baby? That's the best news I've heard all day!' He could have gotten angry or frustrated at having been scammed, or he could have been so angry that he wanted to find this woman. Instead, he accepted what had happened and looked for the positive—there was no sick and dying baby! This doesn't mean he endorsed or approved of her behavior. He simply recognized that it already happened so it can't be otherwise.
