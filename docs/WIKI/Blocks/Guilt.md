# 🧱 Block 4: Guilt ✨

**Guilt** is the emotional result of believing that one has done something "wrong" and that this action makes them a "bad" or "worthless" person.

---

## 🧮 **The Guilt Formula**

### `G = W1 + W2`

- **W1 (Wrongness)**: The idea that an action was bad and "should" not have happened.
- **W2 (Worthlessness)**: The rating of the "Self" based on that action (e.g., "I am a failure").

---

## 🔮 **Core Insight: The BS Equation**

Guilt relies on the **BS Equation (Behavior = Self)**. It is the irrational belief that what you *do* equals who you *are*. 

The rational alternative is **B = B (Behavior = Behavior)**. You can rate your actions (as helpful or unhelpful) without rating your entire essence as a human being.

---

## 📜 **Practical Steps to Dissolve Guilt**

1.  **Accept Fallibility**: Acknowledge that you are an **FHB (Fallible Human Being)**, not a **PHB (Perfect Human Being)**.
2.  **Ditch the Should**: Replace "I should not have done that" with "It was a mistake, and I'd prefer I hadn't done it."
3.  **Rate the Action, Not the Actor**: Use the **B=B** lens to view the mistake as data for future improvement.
4.  **Practice USA**: [[Unconditional Self-Acceptance]].
